Google Search Console — step by step
What this does: Tells Google your site exists, submits your sitemap so all pages get indexed, and gives you data on which search terms bring people to SpellRightPro. Do this today — it can take 1–4 weeks for pages to start appearing in search results, so the sooner you submit the better.
1

Go to Google Search Console

Open search.google.com/search-console and sign in with any Google account.

2

Add a new property

Click Add property → choose URL prefix → enter:

https://spellrightpro.org

Click Continue.

3

Verify ownership — HTML tag method (easiest)

Choose HTML tag verification. Google gives you a meta tag like:

<meta name="google-site-verification" content="XXXX..." />

Add this tag to the <head> of your index.html, deploy to Netlify, then click Verify in Search Console.

4

Submit your sitemap

In the left sidebar go to Sitemaps → enter:

sitemap.xml

Click Submit. Google will crawl all 10 pages in your sitemap.

5

Request indexing for the OET article

In the top search bar of Search Console paste:

https://spellrightpro.org/oet-spelling-guide

Click Request Indexing. This asks Google to crawl the article immediately rather than waiting for its normal schedule.

6

Do the same for Bing (5 extra minutes)

Go to bing.com/webmasters. You can import your Google Search Console property automatically — just sign in with a Microsoft account and click Import from Google Search Console. Takes 2 minutes and covers Bing and DuckDuckGo.

📊 What to check in 2 weeks: Go back to Search Console → Performance → Search results. You'll see which queries are bringing people to the site and which pages are getting impressions. The OET article and freemium-oet page should start appearing. If they're getting impressions but low clicks, the meta descriptions need to be more compelling.
